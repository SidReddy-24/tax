Income Tax Return Filing

Choose the right ITR form based on your income sources and filing requirements. Our expert system will guide you through every step.

ITR-1 (Sahaj)

Simplified return for individuals

Who Should File

Resident individuals with income from:

  • Salary or pension
  • One house property (excluding cases of loss)
  • Other sources (e.g., interest from savings account, fixed deposits)

Important Information

Income limit: Total income up to ₹50 lakh

Not Applicable For

  • Income from business or profession
  • Income from more than one house property

ITR-2

For individuals and HUFs

Who Should File

Individuals or Hindu Undivided Families (HUF) with income from:

  • Salary/pension
  • House property (multiple properties)
  • Capital gains (sale of assets like stocks, property)
  • Other sources (interest income, lottery, etc.)

Important Information

Income limit: No limit, but must be eligible for ITR-2

Not Applicable For

  • Income from business or profession

ITR-3

For individuals and HUFs

Who Should File

Individuals or HUFs with income from:

  • Business or profession
  • Salary/pension
  • House property
  • Capital gains
  • Other sources

Important Information

Applicable to: Those who need to report income from business, freelance work, or self-employment

Not Applicable For

  • Companies or firms

ITR-4 (Sugam)

For individuals, HUFs, and firms

Who Should File

Individuals, Hindu Undivided Families (HUF), and firms (other than LLP) opting for Presumptive Taxation Scheme under:

  • Section 44AD (business turnover up to ₹2 crore)
  • Section 44ADA (professionals with gross receipts up to ₹50 lakh)

Important Information

Presumptive income is calculated at 8% (6% for digital receipts)

Not Applicable For

  • Income from more than one business or profession
  • Businesses not opting for presumptive taxation

ITR-5

For firms, LLPs, AOPs, BOIs, and cooperative societies

Who Should File

Firms, LLPs (Limited Liability Partnerships), Association of Persons (AOPs), Body of Individuals (BOIs), Cooperative societies, Other similar entities

Important Information

Not Applicable For: Individuals or Hindu Undivided Families

ITR-6

For companies (except for those claiming exemption under Section 11 – charitable trusts)

Who Should File

Companies (except for those claiming exemption under Section 11 – charitable trusts), Income from business

Important Information

Not Applicable For: Individuals or firms

ITR-7

For trusts, political parties, charitable institutions, etc.

Who Should File

Trusts, political parties, charitable institutions, etc. that are required to file under:

  • Section 139(4A) – for charitable or religious trusts
  • Section 139(4B) – for political parties
  • Section 139(4C) – for certain institutions like research organizations, etc.

Important Information

Not Applicable For: Individuals or businesses
